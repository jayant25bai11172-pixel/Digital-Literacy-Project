To create this "Digital Literacy Essentials" infographic, I used Canva, a versatile graphic design tool that allows for seamless integration of icons, vibrant gradients, and structured layouts. The design is intended as a high-impact educational resource for students, utilizing a bold, neon-inspired color palette to grab attention while maintaining a professional and organized flow.

The infographic is structured into three distinct sections:

1.Definition: A clear, concise introduction to what digital literacy entails.
2.Student Toolkit: A categorization of essential tools for time management, note-taking, creativity, and research.
3.Safety Protocols: A checklist for maintaining security and privacy online, anchored by a prominent shield icon.

One thing I found particularly interesting during the design process was the challenge of balancing visual hierarchy with information density. I had to experiment with different font weights and icon placements to ensure that the "Safe Internet Practices" section didn't feel cluttered compared to the more minimalist tool icons above. Selecting a background that looked modern and "tech-forward" without making the white text unreadable required fine-tuning the opacity and glow effects on the primary headers.
