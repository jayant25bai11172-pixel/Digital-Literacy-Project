For my professional development over the next four years, I have selected LinkedIn, GitHub, and Kaggle as my primary digital platforms. Each serves a unique purpose in building a "digital twin" of my academic and technical journey.

LinkedIn is my networking hub. I use it to connect with industry professionals, follow tech leaders, and stay updated on market trends. Over the next four years, I plan to transition my profile from a student resume to a professional brand, sharing insights from my coursework and securing internships through its robust job board.

GitHub functions as my "living portfolio." It is where I host the source code for my software projects and collaborate with others using version control. My plan is to maintain a consistent "commit green" streak, moving from simple class assignments in my first year to contributing to major open-source projects by my senior year.

Finally, Kaggle is my training ground for data science. I use it to access diverse datasets and participate in predictive modeling competitions. Throughout my degree, I intend to progress from beginner tutorials to the "Kaggle Rankings," using their cloud-based notebooks to document my growth in machine learning. Together, these platforms will provide a verifiable record of my skills to future employers.
